The problem

Internal search is the least-monitored conversion point on an online store.

The default search engine on most CMS platforms indexes the catalog, but understands neither typos, synonyms, nor the relevance order a buyer expects. The visitor sees a blank page or unrelated results — and closes the tab.

Fixing this doesn't require a new e-commerce platform. It requires a precise audit of what's breaking relevance, then targeted configuration: business synonyms, typo tolerance, sorting by stock and margin, faceted filters that reflect how your customers actually search.

A concrete competitive edge: understanding references, not just words

Most engines — including well-known solutions like Algolia or Elasticsearch when poorly configured — treat a product reference like any other word. Two different formats for the same reference (M8x20 and M8 x 20) are then seen as two unrelated things. On a technical catalog, that's where a meaningful share of lost sales happens — not the site's design, but the engine's ability to understand what the buyer actually typed.

Heurix configures custom regular expression (regex) rules for your catalog: they recognize the structure of a reference — family, dimension, variant — rather than comparing characters one by one. A buyer typing a partial or slightly mis-formatted reference still finds the right product. This kind of fine-tuned configuration work is something few providers offer, because it requires understanding both your catalog and how your customers actually search.

The sectors where this approach changes the most

The larger and more technical a catalog, the more a well-tuned engine matters — because native search fails exactly where fine-grained filtering is needed most.

Tools & hardware

Tens of thousands of references, standardized product codes (DIN, ISO), need to filter by dimension, material, compatibility.

Spare parts & industry

Search by manufacturer reference or equivalence, tolerance for multiple code formats depending on the supplier.

Electrical & electronics

Precise technical specs (voltage, amperage, standards), catalogs that evolve continuously.

Professional equipment & construction

High volumes, professional buyers who search by exact reference more than by descriptive keyword.

    What people ask me the most.

    How much does a mission cost?

    Billed daily, €850/day excl. VAT, quoted based on exact scope. As a guide: an audit alone takes 2 to 3 days, a full mission (audit + integration) costs between €4,250 and €8,500 excl. VAT depending on catalog size. Use the ROI simulator above to estimate the expected gain against this cost.

    What if the results don't show up?

    No consultant can guarantee a percentage gain in advance — the simulator's numbers are order-of-magnitude estimates, not a contractual promise. What limits your risk: no subscription, no long-term commitment. The initial audit (2 to 3 days) gives you a concrete, quantifiable diagnosis before deciding to go further. If the diagnosis doesn't reveal a significant gain, you'll know at that stage, for a limited cost.

    Do I need to change e-commerce platforms?

    No. Heurix works on the search layer of your existing site (Shopify, PrestaShop, WooCommerce, Magento, or other), without touching the rest of your architecture. That's exactly the alternative to a full rebuild: in most cases, the problem is solved by reconfiguring or connecting a search engine, not by switching CMS.

    Proprietary Heurix engine or Algolia / Elasticsearch / Typesense — how do you choose?

    The choice is made during the audit, based on your catalog, your budget, and what you already have in place. If your current solution is simply misconfigured, we fix it — no need to pay for a new tool. If no market solution fits your case (highly technical catalog, need for custom regex, tight budget), the proprietary Heurix engine becomes a relevant option. The goal is never to sell you a default tool.

    How long does a mission take?

    The initial audit takes 2 to 3 days. The integration that follows depends on the scope defined after the diagnostic. Either way, this stays far from a months-long IT project — that's an operating principle, not just a sales pitch.

    Do you work with my industry?

    Heurix is particularly relevant for large, technical catalogs — tools & hardware, spare parts, electrical/electronics, professional equipment/construction in particular (see the Engine section for detail). The principle applies to any site with an underused internal search engine, regardless of sector; these technical catalogs are simply where the gain is clearest.
