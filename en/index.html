A concrete competitive edge: understanding references, not just words

Most engines — including well-known solutions like Algolia or Elasticsearch when poorly configured — treat a product reference like any other word. Two different formats for the same reference (M8x20 and M8 x 20) are then seen as two unrelated things. On a technical catalog, that's where a meaningful share of lost sales happens — not the site's design, but the engine's ability to understand what the buyer actually typed.

Heurix configures custom regular expression (regex) rules for your catalog: they recognize the structure of a reference — family, dimension, variant — rather than comparing characters one by one. A buyer typing a partial or slightly mis-formatted reference still finds the right product. This is fine-grained catalog understanding, encoded once and for all into the engine's rule packs — it takes understanding both your catalog and how your customers actually search.

⚡ You never write a single regex yourself. Our rule packs — hardware, fashion, industry — are pre-configured and activate with your industry choice, at indexing time.

The sectors where this approach changes the most

The larger a catalog or content base, the more a well-tuned search matters — because native search fails exactly where precision is needed most.

Fashion & retail

Compound sizes (W32/L34), colors, materials, variants — and customers who type fast, with typos.

Tools & hardware

Tens of thousands of references, standardized product codes (DIN, ISO), need to filter by dimension, material, compatibility.

Spare parts & industry

Search by manufacturer reference or equivalence, tolerance for multiple code formats depending on the supplier.

Books & culture

ISBNs, editions, formats, collections: the same title exists in dozens of versions — the demo on this page is the proof.

Electrical & electronics

Precise technical specs (voltage, amperage, standards), catalogs that evolve continuously.

Professional equipment & construction

High volumes, professional buyers who search by exact reference more than by descriptive keyword.

Wine & spirits

Vintages, estates, appellations, formats (37.5 cl, magnum): references where every digit matters.

Content, blog & documentation

Articles, FAQ, knowledge base: the same typo tolerance that finds a product reference finds a poorly-worded question. (Try Ctrl+K on this page.)

    Frequently asked questions

    Answers to the questions we get asked.

    How do I integrate the Heurix engine into my site?

    Via API: you index your catalog (one call), you wire up search (one call), you're live. No plugin to install, no rebuild, no dependency on your CMS. The quickstart guide fits in 3 calls. Integration guides with real code are available for Shopify, PrestaShop, WooCommerce, and Magento.

    How much does it cost?

    A 14-day free trial (up to 2,000 requests and 2,000 products) to test without a credit card, then plans from €19/month. Counting rules are public and your usage is available in real time — no end-of-month surprise. Plan details are here.

    Heurix or Algolia / Elasticsearch / Typesense — why pick Heurix?

    Generalist engines compare characters; Heurix reads the structure of your references through its annotation cascade. If your catalog is technical (dimensions, standards, sizes, vintages, editions), that's precisely what Heurix is built for — with rule packs per vertical, stock-aware ranking, and explainable results. For a simple blog or editorial site, a generalist engine is plenty.

    Do I need to change my e-commerce platform?

    No. Heurix plugs into your existing site via API, whatever your CMS — Shopify, PrestaShop, WooCommerce, Magento, or a custom build. Your catalog stays with you; the engine only replaces search.

    How do I measure the impact on my sales?

    The matched field of every result explains why it ranks, and the ROI simulator on this page gives you an order of magnitude based on your traffic and average basket. The most reliable method remains a before/after test on your zero-result queries — measurable in your analytics tool within the first week.

    Is my industry covered?

    Three rule packs ship with the engine (hardware, fashion, industry) and the mechanics apply to any catalog searched by reference, dimension, or variant — books, wine, spare parts, electronics... If your references are very specific, tell us about your catalog: custom packs are available on Scale plans.

    Where is my data hosted?

    In France, at OVH (Gravelines or Strasbourg). Your catalogs never leave the European Union, and the engine itself is designed and built in France. For GDPR requirements, it's the simplest scenario to document with your DPO.

    What happens if I exceed my plan's quota?

    Never an abrupt cutoff: overage is billed at the usage rate shown on the pricing page. Counting rules are public (1 search = 1 request, 1,000 indexed products = 10 requests) and your usage is available in real time via the API.

    Does the demo on this page really use the engine?

    Yes — it's the Heurix engine logic, ported to JavaScript and embedded in the page with a generated demo catalog: same normalization, same typo tolerance, same annotation cascade, same scoring. Developers can query it from the console: window.heurixDemo.search("...").
