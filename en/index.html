A concrete competitive edge: understanding references, not just words

Most engines — including well-known solutions like Algolia or Elasticsearch when poorly configured — treat a product reference like any other word. Two different formats for the same reference (M8x20 and M8 x 20) are then seen as two unrelated things. On a technical catalog, that's where a meaningful share of lost sales happens — not the site's design, but the engine's ability to understand what the buyer actually typed.

Heurix configures custom regular expression (regex) rules for your catalog: they recognize the structure of a reference — family, dimension, variant — rather than comparing characters one by one. A buyer typing a partial or slightly mis-formatted reference still finds the right product. This kind of fine-tuned configuration work is something few providers offer, because it requires understanding both your catalog and how your customers actually search.

"M8 x 20 — A2" becomes DIAM_M8 + LONG_20 + MAT_INOX. Your customers' queries go through the same rules: matching happens on meaning, not spelling.
